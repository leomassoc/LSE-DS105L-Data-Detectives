We start off by retrieving the education data through the Census API (to keep this page unpolluted, we left a detailed and coded explanation in the “Behind the Scenes” page). Initially, we looked at the relation between Bachelor’s degree and GDP p.c. amongst the counties of California. In our research, we also considered the number of educational institutions (kindergarten, primary, middle, high school, colleges, institutes and universities) to measure the investments made by the counties into education.

Data are retrieved from three different datasets and then merged: we used the GEO_ID (the geographical variable used by the census).

Both values were recorded in 2017: unfortunately we are missing longer temporal datasets.
