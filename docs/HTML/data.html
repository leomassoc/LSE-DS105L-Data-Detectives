Comprehensive Data Sources and Analysis for Educational Levels, Salaries, and GDP in U.S. Counties

As part of our research project, we have meticulously gathered data from various authoritative sources to explore educational levels, educational institutions, salaries, and GDP in U.S. counties. The following sections outline the data sources we utilized, the specific datasets we selected, and the tools we employed to enhance the accuracy and comprehensiveness of our findings.

Data Sources

  • GDP:
    • For final data frame: Excel spreadsheet from the BEA (Bureau of Economic Analysis), available here, set for 2017 and for GDP per Capita in each County.
    • For Forecasting Data: CSV file from the World Bank, available here, GDP per capita from 1960 adjusted to 2015 inflation rate. 
    • For race analysis: Excel spreadsheet from the US Department of Labor, available here, used only national level data, and from a limited number of races for a general overview.

  • EDUCATION:
    • For final data frame: API query from the US Census Bureau, available here, set for Educational Firms and percentage of citizens with a Bachelor’s Degree.
    • For Forecasting Data: Excel spreadsheet from the US Census Bureau, available here, set for all ages and sex.
    • For race analysis: Excel spreadsheet from the US Census Bureau, available here, set for both sexes and diversifies by race.
  • UTILITY:
    • Geographical ID code to county/state name converter: TSV and Excel files from Chuck Connel’s public GitHub repository, available here.

    • In order to assess indicators of success in the workforce, we have chosen to utilize GDP per capita (referred to as GDP p.c.) as a reliable measure of individual performance. While we recognize that monetary factors do not encompass the entirety of a fulfilling life and that other subjective elements such as job impact, satisfaction, and popularity are important, they can be challenging to quantify. Consequently, our primary focus will be on GDP per capita as a metric for salary evaluation. To gauge higher education levels, we will rely on data from the Census Bureau pertaining to College/Bachelor's Degrees attainment. This dataset will provide us with a foundational understanding of educational achievement across the country.
